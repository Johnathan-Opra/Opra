05 · Questions

Common questions,
straight answers.

  • What is an operational website?
    An operational website does more than display information. It captures leads and routes them to the right place, connects to your CRM, triggers follow-up sequences, and collects data your team can act on. It replaces manual steps in your sales and onboarding process from the first visit.
  • What types of websites does Opra build?
    Opra builds one-page entry sites, standard business websites, and full operational websites with CRM integration, automation, and lead routing. Every tier includes the operational layer by default. Pricing is scoped per engagement. Book a free consultation to get a fixed quote.
  • How long does a website project take?
    One-page sites take 5 to 7 days. Standard business websites take 2 to 3 weeks. Operational websites with full automation and CRM integration take 3 to 5 weeks. Timelines are scoped and fixed before any work begins.
  • What happens after the site is built?
    You get a full Loom walkthrough and written documentation. Deployment and go-live are handled as part of the project. We discuss ongoing support and retainer options at handover.
