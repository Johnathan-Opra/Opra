The problem

Your recruiters are
recruiting. Admin is
slowing them down.

Recruitment runs on speed and relationships. The operational layer underneath, candidate tracking, client communication, reporting, onboarding, is almost always manual. That costs placements.

Candidate tracking is a spreadsheet. Stages, notes, and status updates live in a shared sheet that five people edit. Things get missed. Duplicates appear. Nobody trusts it.

Client updates are manual. Someone has to compile candidate shortlists, write update emails, and chase feedback. Every week. For every client.

Onboarding new clients takes too long. Brief capture, terms signing, system setup. All done manually and inconsistently depending on who picks up the client.

Reporting eats Friday afternoons. Placement reports, pipeline summaries, and activity stats compiled by hand because there is no system that pulls them automatically.

What we build

Systems built for how recruitment works.

Visibility
Candidate tracking dashboard

A real-time view of every candidate across every role. Stage, status, next action, and owner visible at a glance. No spreadsheet, no chasing.

Client-facing
Client portal

Clients see their shortlists, give feedback, and track placement progress in a secure portal. Reduces back-and-forth and keeps clients engaged between updates.

Automation
Onboarding and reporting

New client briefs trigger an onboarding sequence automatically. Weekly activity reports generated and sent without anyone compiling them.

Questions

Common questions,
straight answers.

  • What systems does Opra build for recruitment firms?
    Opra builds candidate tracking dashboards that give your team real-time visibility over the pipeline, client portals where clients can track placements and approvals, onboarding automation for both new candidates and new clients, and reporting systems that generate placement and activity reports automatically.
  • Can Opra connect to our existing recruitment CRM?
    Yes. We connect to the tools your team already uses. Common recruitment CRMs, Google Workspace, communication tools, and more. The goal is to build on top of your existing setup, not replace it.
  • How does automation help a recruitment firm?
    Automation removes the repetitive manual steps that consume recruiter time: sending candidate updates, chasing client feedback, generating weekly activity reports, routing new job briefs to the right consultant, and following up with candidates who have gone quiet. Every hour saved on admin is an hour a recruiter can spend placing people.
